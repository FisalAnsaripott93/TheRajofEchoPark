About

Fisal Ansari, also known as Sal Pott, is the author of the novel and screenplay The Raj of Echo Park (2025) — a coming-of-age story set in 1993 Echo Park, following a young man navigating a difficult family past. The screenplay was a second-round selection with the Launch Pad competition in 2022.

Fisal is a writer from Echo Park, where he was born and raised on October 30th, 1973, to immigrant parents from India. His parents migrated to Pakistan during the war, then took a plane to Echo Park.

Fisal attended John Marshall High School, graduating in 1993, and went on to receive a bachelor's degree in print journalism and a master's in library science from the University of Arizona at Tucson. He has worked as a writer for public television station KCET-TV alongside Huell Howser and for PBS News, and has been published in the San Fernando Business Journal and The Sun newspaper. He has studied writing since 2009 with Pilar Alessandra's On the Page writing studio, Corey Mandell's writing studio, and the UCLA Extension Writers' Program.

The Raj of Echo Park

Logline

In 1993 Echo Park, a charismatic but emotionally repressed Indo-Pakistani teen with a deep love for classic cinema must defy his hardline police officer father and face down a neighborhood bully to pursue his dream of becoming a Hollywood actor — all while trying to win over his true love, Brooklyn.

Synopsis

The Raj of Echo Park follows Salman "Sal" Khan, a 19-year-old high school senior whose movie-quoting bravado masks a deep-seated emotional wound inflicted by his father, a Pakistani immigrant and LAPD officer. Sal dreams of breaking into Hollywood, spending his days at Grauman's Chinese Theatre, where he lands a job as an usher. He's in love with Brooklyn Rotolo, his high school sweetheart, and spends his time in Ms. Adrian's drama class acting out scenes with her.

The emotional core of the story is Sal's relationship with his father — a traumatic childhood incident involving a hot kitchen knife and a lesson in "being a man" has left Sal terrified of his father's disapproval.

Sal is given the chance to audition for the lead role in a Walt Disney feature and must prove to producer and acting coach Lou Sacks that he's got what it takes. But between his PTSD, an impending Marine Corps enlistment forced on him by his father, a rivalry with a local gang member, and the emotional intimacy Brooklyn demands, Sal finds solace only in his craft.

A profoundly moving story of family, history, and the meaning of home, The Raj of Echo Park is both a gripping page-turner and a sensitive family story — exploring culture clash, identity, Hollywood excess, interracial love, and the rifts within a family, uncovering the ways fathers, sons, and mothers struggle to understand one another.
